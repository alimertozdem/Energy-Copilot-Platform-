# Founder Intro Video — Script

**Duration target:** 90-120 seconds
**Format:** Self-recorded (phone or webcam, landscape)
**Language:** English
**Tone:** Confident, technical-but-accessible, passion-driven

---

## SETUP CHECKLIST (before recording)

- [ ] Quiet room, no AC/fan noise
- [ ] Window light on your face (not behind you)
- [ ] Phone or laptop on tripod (or stack of books), camera at eye level
- [ ] Lavalier mic if you have one (otherwise phone mic 30cm from your mouth)
- [ ] Clean background (bookshelf, plain wall, NO clutter)
- [ ] Wear a solid-color shirt (avoid stripes/patterns)
- [ ] Glass of water nearby (clear throat before takes)
- [ ] Phone on silent
- [ ] Test recording 30 seconds → check audio + lighting
- [ ] Have script visible (printed or on second screen, behind camera)

## THE SCRIPT

### Part 1 — HOOK (15 seconds)

*[Camera on you. Direct eye contact. Slight smile.]*

> **"Did you know that commercial buildings in Europe produce 36 percent of all CO2 emissions on the continent? And yet — most of them are still managed with monthly utility bills and Excel spreadsheets."**

*[Pause for 1 second. Slight head shake.]*

> **"That's the problem I'm solving."**

---

### Part 2 — PROBLEM + SOLUTION (40 seconds)

*[Calmer tone, confident.]*

> **"Hi, I'm Mert. I'm an Energy Engineer from Turkey, currently finishing my Master's in Energy Management in Berlin."**

*[Slight gesture.]*

> **"While studying EU energy regulations, I realized something striking: the European Union has rolled out aggressive climate rules — CRREM pathways, the EnEfG energy efficiency law, the Battery Regulation — and property managers are completely unprepared. They face billions of euros in compliance risk, and most don't even have real-time visibility into their own buildings' energy use."**

*[Brief pause.]*

> **"So I built EnergyLens — a Microsoft Fabric-native energy intelligence platform that gives property managers a live, multi-building dashboard with built-in EU compliance scoring. Anomaly detection in real time. Battery dispatch ROI scenarios. HVAC retrofit prioritization. All in one place, accessible to mid-market customers — not just enterprise."**

---

### Part 3 — WHY ME + VISION (35 seconds)

*[Lean in slightly. Stronger conviction.]*

> **"What makes this different? Three things."**

*[Quick count on fingers, optional.]*

> **"First — I'm Microsoft DP-600 certified, with deep expertise in Microsoft Fabric. Our entire platform is built natively on Fabric — that's a strategic moat."**

> **"Second — energy engineering plus energy management means I understand the domain. I'm not a generic data person trying to learn buildings. I AM the customer."**

> **"And third — I'm in Berlin. The DACH region is the most regulated, most demanding energy market in Europe. We're not bolting on EU features later — they're at the core."**

*[Brief pause, then slightly faster — execution proof line.]*

> **"And in the last three weeks, solo, I shipped the live web application AND an AI Copilot that reasons over the Microsoft Fabric Lakehouse using tool use. Not a prototype — a working system you can sign into today."**

*[Brief pause. Final line, slightly slower.]*

> **"With Microsoft for Startups support — especially Azure OpenAI credits to take the Copilot from Mock to production — EnergyLens can become the default Fabric-native energy platform for European commercial buildings. I'd love to be part of the program."**

*[Slight smile. Hold for 1-2 seconds. Cut.]*

## DELIVERY TIPS

### Voice
- **Pace:** Slightly slower than normal conversation
- **Inflection:** Rise at end of "problem" statements, fall at end of "solution" statements
- **Volume:** Normal conversational level, not too loud, not too quiet

### Body language
- **Eye contact:** Look INTO the camera lens, not at your screen
- **Hands:** Visible occasionally for emphasis, don't hide them under the table
- **Posture:** Sit up straight, slight forward lean shows engagement
- **Face:** Soft smile (not big grin), serious but warm

### Common mistakes to avoid
- ❌ Reading word-for-word (sounds robotic)
- ❌ "Umm" / "uhh" / "şey" — pause silently instead
- ❌ Looking at notes between sentences
- ❌ Touching face/hair (distracts)
- ❌ Sitting too far from mic (audio sounds thin)
- ❌ Speaking too fast when nervous

## RECORDING WORKFLOW

1. **Practice 3 times without recording** — get comfortable with flow
2. **Record Take 1** — likely rough, that's OK
3. **Record Take 2** — usually best (warmed up + still fresh)
4. **Record Take 3** — backup, slight variation
5. **Watch all takes back** — pick the strongest
6. **If unsatisfied** — wait 30 minutes, try again with rested voice

## 4. Inbound → delivery workflow + templates

**First reply (paste, English):**
> "Thanks for reaching out. To scope this I need: (1) 12 months of utility bills (PDF/Excel) per
> building, (2) a building list (address, type, floor area, year if known), (3) any EPCs you have.
> From that I'll return [Dashboard / EPBD-readiness report] within [3–5 / 5–10] working days. Fixed
> price €[…]; working language English, report available in German. Shall I send a short scope note?"

**Data-request checklist:** bills (12 mo) · building list (type, m², year) · EPCs · tariff if known ·
any meter exports. *(Missing data is fine — the model estimates with stated ranges.)*

**Delivery checklist (before it leaves):**
1. **QA every number** — sanity-check against plausible ranges (this sample caught a misread bill and
   sub-year paybacks; do the same each time).
2. Anonymise nothing for a real client (it's their data) — but for *portfolio samples/case studies*,
   anonymise.
3. Captions + footer disclaimer present.
4. Ranges + assumptions stated; no single-point savings claims.

## 5. Engagement terms (plain-language — not legal advice; have a lawyer review for real contracts)

Paste into the proposal:
- **Scope:** [Dashboard / EPBD-readiness screening] for [N] buildings, from supplied bill/EPC/building data.
- **Nature:** decision-support / screening — **not** a certified Energieausweis, iSFP, or audit.
- **Price / turnaround:** €[…] fixed, [N] working days. Language: English (German report on request).
- **Reuse / reference clause (do not skip):** *"Provider may use anonymised, aggregated results of
  this engagement as a reference/case study, and retains all rights to its platform and methods."*
  Without this you legally cannot use the work as a portfolio reference.
- **Data:** client supplies data; provider handles it confidentially and deletes on request.

---

## 6. "Do it right" guardrails

- **QA before send** — every figure. Credibility is the whole asset.
- **Ranges + assumptions**, never false precision.
- **No overpromise** — advertise only what the package can fulfil.
- **English-first; don't pose as German-native** (B1). German written reports OK; German calls not committed.
- **Screening ≠ certified** — say it on every deliverable.

# Job cover letter — structure & tone (JOB stream)

This is the skeleton for LinkedIn / employment JOB postings. Output is a short,
natural cover letter Mert pastes into a cover-letter field or sends to a recruiter.

## Tone — read this first
Write like a real person applying for a job — warm, plain, and honest. NOT like
an AI and NOT like a stiff template.
- Simple, natural English. Short paragraphs. No corporate filler.
- NO AI clichés / buzzwords. Banned: "I am excited to apply", "I would be a
  great fit", "passionate", "leverage", "robust", "seamless", "proven track
  record", "hit the ground running", "team player", "dynamic environment",
  "I am confident that", "thrilled".
- Honest, early-career framing. Mert is finishing an M.Sc. and has ~1 year of
  energy-engineering work + a strong personal project. Don't pretend to be senior.
- Use real facts from the personal context only (don't invent experience).

## Structure (keep it short — 150–210 words)
1. Open with the specific role + one honest sentence on why it interests him
   (tie to energy + data, his actual focus).
2. What he brings: energy/ESG data turned into Power BI / Microsoft Fabric
   dashboards (energy KPIs, Scope 1/2/3, anomaly detection), Microsoft Certified
   Fabric Analytics Engineer (DP-600). One short paragraph, concrete not boastful.
3. One or two lines of real background: energy-engineering work on solar PV
   (Elegant Enerji) and a wind O&M internship (Enercon), M.Sc. Energy Management
   in Berlin, and EnergyLens — a personal end-to-end Fabric/Power BI project he
   built to go deep on the stack.
4. A brief, genuine line on why this company/role fits what he wants.
5. A simple, polite close — open to a short conversation. Sign:  Best regards, Ali Mert Özdemir

## Rules
- Plain text only. No markdown, no emojis, no bold.
- This is a JOB application (employment), not a freelance pitch — no prices,
  no packages, no "hire me for a gig" language.
- Don't claim certifications, tools, or experience not in the personal context.

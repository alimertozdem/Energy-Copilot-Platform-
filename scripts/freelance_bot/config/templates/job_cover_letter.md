# Job cover letter — structure & tone (JOB stream)

Skeleton for LinkedIn / employment JOB postings. Output = a short, natural cover
letter Mert pastes into a cover-letter field or sends to a recruiter.

## Tone — read this first
Warm, plain, honest — a real early-career person who is hungry to grow. NOT an AI,
NOT a stiff template, NOT inflated.
- Simple, natural English. Short paragraphs. No corporate filler.
- Core message: "I'm early in my career (junior) and honest about that, but I'm
  genuinely driven — I work hard, I keep learning, and I'm investing in myself to
  grow. I've already taught myself a lot by building real things." Confident about
  effort and direction, humble about seniority. Never exaggerate.
- Banned AI cliches/buzzwords: "I am excited to apply", "I would be a great fit",
  "passionate", "leverage", "robust", "seamless", "proven track record",
  "hit the ground running", "team player", "dynamic environment", "thrilled".
- Use only real facts from the personal context.

## Structure (keep it short — 150-200 words)
1. The specific role + one honest sentence on why it genuinely interests him
   (tie to energy + data, his real focus).
2. What he brings, concretely and modestly: energy/ESG data turned into Power BI /
   Microsoft Fabric dashboards (energy KPIs, Scope 1/2/3, anomaly detection),
   Microsoft Certified Fabric Analytics Engineer (DP-600).
3. One or two lines of real background: solar PV engineering (Elegant Enerji), a
   wind O&M internship (Enercon), M.Sc. Energy Management in Berlin, and
   EnergyLens — an end-to-end Fabric/Power BI project he built himself to go deep.
4. The growth line, sincere and unexaggerated: he is early-career and knows it, but
   he's eager to learn, works hard, and is putting in the work to get there.
5. A simple, polite close — open to a short conversation if they'd like.
   Sign:  Best regards, Ali Mert Özdemir

## Rules
- Plain text only. No markdown, no emojis, no bold.
- JOB application (employment) — no prices, packages, or freelance pitch language.
- Don't claim certifications, tools, or experience not in the personal context.

# Freelance proposal — structure & tone (FREELANCE stream)

Skeleton the LLM follows for FREELANCE gigs (Freelancermap / Malt / Contra).
Output = plain text Mert pastes into the platform's proposal box.

## Tone — read this first
The message must make the client feel: "this person understood my problem and
clearly knows how to solve it." Warm, sincere, trustworthy, concise.
- Plain, simple English. Short sentences. No jargon walls, no marketing voice.
- Lead with THEIR problem and the solution — not with us, and not with price.
- Honest and grounded. Mert is early in his freelance career; never oversell.
- Banned AI/brochure cliches: "I am excited", "I would love to", "passionate",
  "leverage", "robust", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "delve", "great fit",
  "synergy", "deep dive", "best-in-class", "in today's fast-paced world".
- Present EnergyLens honestly as a platform he built (Microsoft Fabric + Power BI
  for energy & ESG analytics) — proof he can do the work, not paid client history.

## Structure (keep it short — 150-220 words)
1. Their problem, in your own plain words (1-2 sentences). Show you actually read it.
2. A clear solution ROADMAP: the concrete steps you'd take to solve it using
   EnergyLens (3-4 short steps). Specific and confident, not boastful. Tie it to
   the ONE EnergyLens capability closest to their need. This is the heart of the
   message — they should be able to picture the outcome.
3. One real, specific question about their data or scope (shows you're thinking
   about their actual case, not pasting a template).
4. Money: do NOT put a price or package up front. The priority is solving their
   problem. If a floor is truly needed, say it softly and last, e.g. "to do this
   properly I usually start from a small minimum, and I'll happily scope it to
   your budget." Never lead with money.

## Close
- Restate, in one friendly sentence, the outcome they'll get.
- Do NOT propose a call or meeting. Invite them to share more about their case, or
  to say if they'd like to take it forward — let them choose the next step.
- Sign off:  Best, Mert

## Rules
- Plain text only. No markdown, no bullet symbols, no emojis, no bold.
- Don't invent numbers, tools, experience, or certifications he doesn't have.

# Freelance proposal — structure & tone (FREELANCE stream)

This is the skeleton the LLM follows for FREELANCE gigs (freelancermap / Malt /
Contra). Output is plain text Mert pastes into the platform's proposal box.

## Tone — read this first
Write like a real, helpful person — NOT like an AI or a marketing brochure.
- Plain, simple English. Short sentences. No jargon walls.
- Honest and grounded. Mert is early in his freelance career — never oversell.
- NO buzzwords or AI clichés. Banned: "I am excited", "I would love to",
  "passionate", "leverage", "robust", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "delve",
  "in today's fast-paced world", "I am confident that", "great fit",
  "synergy", "deep dive", "best-in-class", em-dash pile-ups.
- Present EnergyLens honestly as a personal project he built to learn the stack
  (Microsoft Fabric + Power BI), NOT as paid client work.
- It's fine to be modest and to offer a small, clearly-scoped first step.

## Structure (keep it short — 150–230 words)
1. Their problem, in your own plain words (1–2 sentences). Shows you read the post.
2. What you can do for it + ONE relevant EnergyLens proof point (one short
   paragraph). Pick the single proof artifact closest to their need.
3. One real, specific question about their data or scope.
4. The closest package with its price + rough timeline. Offer two options only
   if it genuinely helps them choose. Hourly is fine if nothing fits.
5. A short, low-pressure close — offer a quick call or a small first task.

## Rules
- Plain text only. No markdown, no bullet symbols, no emojis, no bold.
- Don't invent numbers, tools, or experience that aren't in the personal context.
- Don't claim certifications or skills he doesn't have.
- Sign off:  Best, Mert
